Classic Yellow Cake with chocolate frosting

This fluffy vanilla cake recipe is the absolute best yellow cake from scratch and paired with the silky smooth chocolate frosting, you can't beat it for a classic birthday cake or any other celebration!

Are you a box mix yellow cake person? Or a from-scratch yellow cake person?

Truth be told, it's REALLY hard to replicate that always-moist-and-fluffy-texture that everyone loves about a box cake mix, but at the same time, we also love the homemade, right?!

It took me a long time to find a homemade yellow cake that I really, really love. Box cakes mixes seem to always bake up perfectly fluffy and moist yellow cakes, and the one I've had from bakeries have
been great as well, but I just couldn't seem to replicate it at home no matter how many recipes I tried. (Consequently, I haven't had the same issue with chocolate cakes! See: Snicker-Cake - the base recipe for
all of my chocolate cakes!).

Many were too dense, to dry, too chiffon-like, too fussy in their preparation. I just wanted a fantastic yellow cake recipe I could use for birthday cakes that was easy to make and turned out delicious. I came
across this recipe from Joy while I was on the hunt for yet another recipe to test cut for Joseph's birthday.

This was IT. The recipe that had eluded me for years!

I love that this can easily be mixed together in on bowl using my hand mixer; there's nothing fussy about the ingredients or preparation, and it produces an absolutely perfect yellow cake. The layers are light
but sturdy, buttery and moist, and the ideal for your very favorite chocolate frosting.

I used my chocolate frosting recipe from my ultimate chocolate cupcakes, and it was a wonderful companion to this cake. The frosting is more buttery than sugary, satiny smooth and full of rich chocolate flavour.
It glides on like a dream!

Yellow cake tends to be the elusive unicorn of homemade baking; so many cakes can turn out too dry, too crumbly, too dense, too cornbread-like, and I have one major piece of advice for ensuring yellow cakes
perfection:

Make sure all of the ingredients listed "at room temperatured" ARE ACTUALLY AT ROOM TEMPERATURE.

I've totally been there and have used stll-cool butter, eggs or dairy in a recipe when I'm in a hungry, but it's actually vitally important here. Having all of those ingredients at room temperature ensures that they
emulsify, which traps air in the batter and, once in the oven, theat air expands and produces a fluffy cake.

Room temperature ingredients also ensure that the batter comes together well and is ultra-smooth, which keeps the resulting cake from being crumbly or cornbread-like.
